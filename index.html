DETERMINATION OF TOTAL HARDNESS OF A SAMPLE OF WATER

Let's Begin!

Explore the titration process and identify the apparatus used in the experiment.

Experiment Procedure

Part A: Preparation of Standard EDTA Solution

  1. Weigh the weighing bottle containing the required amount of disodium salt of EDTA using an electronic balance and record the weight.
  2. Transfer the EDTA crystals carefully into a 250 mL volumetric flask using a funnel.
  3. Weigh the empty weighing bottle again and record the weight to determine the exact amount of EDTA used.
  4. Add ion-exchange water through the funnel to ensure all crystals are transferred into the flask.
  5. Wash the funnel with ion-exchange water to transfer any remaining crystals into the flask.
  6. Remove the funnel and gently swirl the flask to dissolve the EDTA crystals completely.
  7. Add ion-exchange water to the flask until the solution reaches the calibration mark.
  8. Stopper the flask and mix thoroughly by inverting it several times to obtain a homogeneous solution.

Part B: Determination of Total Hardness of Water Sample

  1. Pipette out 25 mL of the given water sample into a clean conical flask.
  2. Add 3 mL of NH₃–NH₄Cl buffer solution to the flask.
  3. Add a pinch of Eriochrome Black T indicator to the solution.
  4. Observe that the solution turns wine red in color.
  5. Titrate the solution with the standard EDTA solution from the burette.
  6. Continue titration until the color changes from wine red to clear blue (end point).
  7. Record the volume of EDTA used.
  8. Repeat the titration to obtain consistent (concordant) readings.
